History of MTG

A simple and short overview of the history on MTG.

Back to Card Search

1993 - The Beginning

Magic: The Gathering was created by Richard Garfield and released by Wizards of the Coast in 1993. It is widely recognized as the first major trading card game.

1993 - Alpha and Early Success

The first release of the game was Alpha. It sold quickly and helped establish the core structure of deckbuilding, collectible cards, and strategic one-on-one play that would define Magic going forward.

1993 - The First Expansion

Later in 1993, Arabian Nights became the game’s first expansion set. This started Magic’s long pattern of adding new mechanics, themes, and settings through regular releases.

1994 to 1995 - Rapid Growth

During its early years, Magic expanded very quickly. More sets were released, more cards were printed, and local stores and tournaments helped build a growing player community.

1996 - Organized Competitive Play

The first Pro Tour in 1996 helped establish Magic as a serious competitive game. Organized play became a major part of the game’s long-term identity.

Late 1990s and 2000s - Expanding the Game

As the game matured, Magic added more worlds, mechanics, and storylines. Set design became more structured, and the game developed a stronger long-term identity.

2002 - Magic Online

Magic Online launched in 2002 and gave players a digital way to play the game while still keeping many of the same rules and competitive ideas as tabletop Magic.

2010s - Modern Magic

During the 2010s, Magic continued to grow through new mechanics, stronger brand identity, and the rise of formats such as Commander, which became especially popular with casual players.

2018 - MTG Arena

MTG Arena entered open beta in 2018 and became one of the most important digital platforms for the game, helping Magic reach a wider audience online.

Magic Today

Today, Magic: The Gathering exists as both a tabletop and digital game. It continues to grow through new sets, local and competitive play, and digital platforms such as MTG Arena.
